Growth doesn't stall randomly. It stalls at predictable bottlenecks — and after working with over 120 companies across Belgium, we see the same five patterns repeat.

## 1. Flying Blind

Most scale-ups have data somewhere, but it's fragmented across spreadsheets, ad platforms, and CRM exports. Without a single source of truth, every growth decision becomes a debate instead of a data-driven choice.

**What to do:** Start with tracking and attribution. You can't optimize what you can't measure.

## 2. Referral Dependency

Many Belgian scale-ups grow through word-of-mouth and referrals — which works until it doesn't. When referral volume plateaus, there's no backup engine.

**What to do:** Build a demand generation system that runs independently of your network.

## 3. Leaky Funnel

Generating leads is only half the battle. We regularly see companies with healthy top-of-funnel numbers but conversion rates below 5%. The problem isn't demand — it's what happens after the lead comes in.

**What to do:** Map your funnel, implement lead scoring, and automate follow-up.

## 4. Heroic Sales

When growth depends on one or two star performers closing deals through relationships and hustle, you don't have a growth engine — you have a dependency.

**What to do:** Document what works, build playbooks, and create systems that any team member can execute.

## 5. Scaling Chaos

The final bottleneck hits when growth itself creates complexity. More leads, more deals, more tools — but no processes to manage it all.

**What to do:** Invest in RevOps, forecasting, and a commercial operating model before the chaos becomes permanent.
